Organization Profile

Rumah Anak Yatim / Rumah Amal Raudhatul Jannah Banting

Rumah Amal Raudhatul Jannah is presented in this website as a welfare-focused children’s home in Banting, Selangor. The purpose of this page is to introduce the home’s role, values, and possible support needs to visitors and potential donors.

The purpose of presenting this information is to help the public understand the home respectfully and responsibly before planning donations, visits, or volunteer activities.

Background

About The Home

A children’s care home plays an important role in providing care, supervision, and support for children who need a stable and guided environment. According to Kebajikan Masyarakat (JKM), care centres may provide care for children, persons with disabilities, women, elderly people, and other target groups. JKM also states that safety compliance is an important requirement for registered care centres under the Care Centres Act 1993 [Act 506].

At A Glance

Rumah Amal Raudhatul Jannah operates as a registered welfare home providing shelter, education support, and moral guidance to children in need.

  • Established: 2015
  • Registered With: Jabatan Kebajikan Masyarakat (JKM)
  • Registration No. (ROS): PPM-006-10-26032015
  • Current Capacity: Approximately 30–40 children
  • Core Focus: Academic support, religious classes, and community engagement
  • Location: Kampung Olak Lempit, Banting, Selangor

Purpose

Mission And Objectives

Mission

To provide a caring and supportive environment where children can receive shelter, education support, moral guidance, and encouragement for a better future.

Vision

To become a trusted welfare home supported by responsible donors, visitors, and volunteers who contribute with sincerity, transparency, and respect.

Values

Care, trust, responsibility, respect, education, safety, and community cooperation.

Our Commitment

Beyond shelter, we provide a nurturing ecosystem designed to help each child reach their full potential. Our holistic approach ensures no child is left behind.

  1. Academic support and tuition assistance.
  2. Balanced daily meals and nutritional care.
  3. Regular health screenings and medical support.
  4. Character development through religious and moral guidance.
  5. Recreational and creative activities to foster teamwork and confidence.
